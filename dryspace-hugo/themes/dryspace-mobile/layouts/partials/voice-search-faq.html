What causes water in my basement?

Water enters basements through foundation cracks, poor drainage, high water tables, or failed waterproofing systems. In Toronto, clay soil expansion and spring thaw are the most common causes.

The main causes include:

  1. Hydrostatic Pressure: Water in soil pushes against foundation walls
  2. Foundation Cracks: Concrete settling creates entry points
  3. Poor Grading: Water flows toward instead of away from house
  4. Clogged Weeping Tiles: Drainage system failure
  5. Window Well Problems: Water accumulation around basement windows

Can I waterproof my basement myself?

While minor moisture issues can be DIY projects, professional waterproofing is recommended for serious water problems. DIY solutions rarely address the root cause and may void your home insurance.

DIY Options (for minor issues only):

  • Applying waterproof paint (temporary solution)
  • Cleaning gutters and downspouts
  • Improving grading around foundation
  • Sealing small cracks with hydraulic cement

Professional Required for:

  • Active water leaks
  • Structural foundation cracks
  • Interior/exterior drainage systems
  • Sump pump installation
  • Warranty-backed solutions

What's the difference between interior and exterior waterproofing?

Interior waterproofing manages water that enters your basement, while exterior waterproofing prevents water from reaching your foundation walls. Both methods are effective, but serve different purposes.

Interior Waterproofing

  • Installs drainage system inside basement
  • Less invasive and more affordable
  • Can be done year-round
  • Manages hydrostatic pressure
  • Ideal for finished basements

Exterior Waterproofing

  • Excavates and seals outside walls
  • More comprehensive but costly
  • Weather dependent installation
  • Prevents water entry completely
  • Best for new construction
